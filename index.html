The 18th International Congress on Logistics and SCM Systems

Supply Chain in an Uncertain Era – A Cross Disciplines Collaboration

August 20-23, 2024

Hosted by National Taiwan Ocean University

Keelung, Taiwan

Join us in 2024 at National Taiwan Ocean University

The early 2020s are an era of economic, political, environmental, hygienic and regulatory uncertainty and instability which result in a great challenge to global supply chain management. How to overcome these challenges via a systematically research to find the best practices to smoothen and maintain the regular global trade required cross-disciplines collaboration from the academics and the logistics industry. Prolonged economic conflict between major nations, two regional wars in the Europe and Middle East, the interrupting occurrence of coronavirus, and the EU ends block exemption for shipping all are significant issues for the logistics academicians and supply chain managers. The insourcing of manufacturing industry in the North America and Western Europe, the diversion of cargo ships via the Cape Horn and Cape Hope, the replacement of labor by autonomous and intelligent machine, and the de-alliance of two leading ocean container carriers are all the responses to the challenges. All of the responses come with repercussion impacts. This conference aims to get together the logistics and supply chain talent from the industry and academics to exchange ideas and work together across various disciplines to smoothing and sustaining global supply chain operations.
